Central Government Dashboard

Budget allocations and transfers to Local Councils have been consistently low throughout the years, showing a weak commitment to decentralisation. Low budget affects the capacity of local councils to provide quality services.

Decentralisation policy requires the central government to make quarterly transfers to local councils. There have been consistent delays as reflected in the baseline 2023.

Sierra Leone has implemented decentralisation for 20 years without a Fiscal Devolution law. A good fiscal decentralisation policy (revenue sharing arrangement) could potentially increase resource allocation/budget transfers to local councils.
